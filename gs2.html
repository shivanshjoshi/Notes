  3. Nationalism and Crisis of Federalism: 3 Types of Nationalism - 1930's
    1. Cultural or Ethno-Religious: Hindu- Muslim, Bifurcation of Muslim league and Hindu Mahasabha. Sense of being from one religion overweight their mother tongue and place. -> Prevail after Partition
    2. Civic: Based on common struggle in history against British -> Constitution adopted with this federal structure
    3. Coalescent with Linguistic federalism: Regional, territorial distinct from others but of same state/ place along with lingustic federal. -> Thought to lead to Break ups in new and fragile India.
    1953's:
    Linguistic states focus of popular agitation -> Creation of Andra pradesh => Looks after all major linguistic group, Administration was easy, deepens democracy and alleviates anxiety of regional minorities
    1956: Coalescent seems helpful and effective for democray:
    1953 states reorganised -> India coelescent nation state. From "Holding together" to "Coming Together"
    Benefits of Coelescent: Created strong regional parties -> Centre now more focused towards them and Centre-state relation improved -> Treating all states similar.
